Follow the Steps

GATHER ALL THE MATERIALS YOU'LL NEED
Step 1

This tutorial requires you to have a basic understanding of circuits for you to be able to make sense of the materials you'll need. As you've seen above, you'll need an arduino (and the accompanying wires), a breadboard, resistors, capacitors, inductors, and diodes. Here's the link for the shopping cart with all the materials to recreate this project twice (or if you just messed up the first time). I did end up breaking both the inductors.

Fair word of warning however: You will need to solder wires onto the connecting ends of the inductor. The inductors are tiny and it is difficult to solder so take your time. In the shopping cart, I have added two inductors, however feel free to add more; they are only $0.50 each.

Without further ado here is the link.




DOWNLOAD THE SOFTWARE
Step 2

This project requires equal contribution from software and hardware. Once you've placed an order for the hardware part, go ahead and download the software needed to familiarize yourself with it. First, go to the website for all things arduino and download the arduino software.

Once that's done, download ChucK. ChucK is a music programming language that enables people to code music. Since we are modulating water, you would obviously need to download the software for ChucK as well. Go here and download it for the appropriate OS.

Then, go ahead and download this file which contains all the relevant ChucK code. Take some time out and familiarize yourselves with the commands reqiured to run the program.




CREATING THE CIRCUIT
Step 3

Undoubtably, the hardest part of the project, be sure to closely look at the photos provided to create the circuit. First things first, label all the resistors, capacitors, inductors, and diodes for easy reference.

Create the circuit on the breadboard that you have. A few of the wires have to connect from the breadboard to the arduino to create a complete circuit.

Once you're done creating the curcuit, take the alligator clip you have and attach it to one end of the arduino. The other end has to be dipped into the cup of water.

Go ahead and get a cup of water and dip the other end of the clip in.

EXPLORE THE ARDUINO
Step 4

Now that you have created the circuit, the next part is to test everything out. First, check if the circuit has been done properly by plugging in the arduino with the cable that comes with the arduino. It should light up (the arduino equivalent of 'Hello World.')

To open the arduino software, you can directly go into the mug-music folder you downloaded and open the file in 'touche_peak.' Once opened, just press on upload (little arrow in the menu bar).

Take the alligator clip you have and attach it to one end of the arduino. The other end has to be dipped into the cup of water. Once this is done, press on serial monitor in the menu bar, and dip your finger in the water to test whether there is some change in activity.

EXPLORE CHUCK
Step 5

If the arduino works fine, then its time to test out the ChucK program. There are two ways you can run it. Both are described in the README markdown provided in the folder. I personally prefer running it via the terminal; it is less confusing.

Anyway, once you have it running (congratulations! you made music :D) try out a variety of things.

  • Dip your finger in. Or dip multiple fingers in.
  • Swirl your finger in the water.
  • Hold the cup.
  • Hold the cup and firmly put your hand on your laptop. DISCLAIMER: your lapotp has to be placed firmly on a flat surface for most effect!
  • Put your finger in the water and your other hand firmly on your laptop.
  • Try different vessels such as wine glasses, a shallow tub, or a ceramic mug. See if you can get it to behave differently with different carriers.
  • Will two alligator clips (two mugs work) as well?

    • EXTRAS
    Step 6

    As an extra, you can also run the simple chuck program and see how it behaves when you dip your finger into the water. It is merely a sin wave program; the simplest we can write in ChucK to see its functionality.

    Close the ChucK program and head back to the arduino one. Go to Tools, and select Serial Plotter - it should open up a graph. Dip your finger into the water and see how that renders on the graph.
